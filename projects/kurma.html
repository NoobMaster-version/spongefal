Project Overview

Kurma is a turtle-inspired quadruped robot built for the prestigious international Mass Robotics competition. Named after the turtle avatar in Hindu mythology, this robot embodies the steady, determined movement of its namesake while incorporating advanced robotics technology.

The Competition Challenge

The Mass Robotics competition required teams to build quadruped robots capable of:

  • Stable walking on various terrains
  • Precise gait control and coordination
  • Autonomous navigation capabilities
  • Robust mechanical design for competition tasks
  • Real-time response to environmental challenges

Development at SRA Lab

The entire development and refinement process took place at SRA Lab, where I:

  • Designed and tested multiple gait patterns
  • Refined mechanical components for optimal performance
  • Debugged control algorithms through iterative testing
  • Optimized power consumption for competition duration
  • Collaborated with team members for system integration

Gait Mechanism Engineering

  • Turtle-Inspired Movement: Studied natural turtle locomotion for biomimetic design
  • Stability Analysis: Ensured static stability throughout walking cycle
  • Coordination Control: Synchronized four legs for smooth movement
  • Adaptive Gait: Multiple gait patterns for different terrains and speeds

Engineering Challenges

  • Gait Stability: Developed stable walking patterns that prevent tipping and ensure smooth locomotion
  • Servo Coordination: Created precise timing algorithms to coordinate multiple servo motors
  • Mechanical Durability: Designed robust joints and connections to withstand competition stress
  • Power Efficiency: Optimized control algorithms to maximize battery life during operation

Competition Achievement

Kurma successfully advanced through multiple levels of the international Mass Robotics competition. The robot's stable gait mechanism and reliable performance impressed judges and demonstrated the effectiveness of biomimetic design principles in robotics.
