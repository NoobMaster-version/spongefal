Project Overview

An automated pet feeder built for the SRA lab's cat to ensure regular feeding during off-hours and holidays. The project is expanding to help local animal welfare NGOs with more units.

Feeder in Action

The feeder in action - dispensing food automatically

How It Works

The automated pet feeder operates on a simple yet effective mechanism:

  • Timer-based food dispensing at regular intervals
  • Servo motor rotates to release controlled portions
  • Food drops through dispensing chute into feeding bowl
  • System operates continuously without human intervention

Design Philosophy

This project addresses a real need in our community. The SRA lab has a resident cat that needed regular feeding, especially during holidays and off-hours when students aren't around. The solution needed to be:

  • Reliable and low-maintenance
  • Easy to refill and operate
  • Cost-effective using available materials
  • Scalable for deployment to animal welfare NGOs

Technical Components

The feeder uses simple but effective components to ensure reliable operation:

  • SRA Board: Main microcontroller for timing and control logic
  • Servo Motor: Precise food dispensing mechanism
  • 3D Printed Parts: Custom housing and dispensing components
  • Storage Container: Large capacity food reservoir
  • Power Supply: Reliable power system for continuous operation

Community Impact

What started as a solution for our lab cat has grown into a broader initiative:

  • Ensures regular feeding for animals during holidays and weekends
  • Reduces dependency on human schedules for animal care
  • Plans to donate units to local animal welfare NGOs
