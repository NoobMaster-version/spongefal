River Cleaning Robot

Project Overview

Created for my Environmental Studies course during third semester, this aquatic robot addresses the critical issue of river pollution. The boat-based system is designed to collect floating trash and debris from water bodies, helping restore our rivers to their natural state.

Environmental Challenge

River pollution is a major environmental crisis affecting:

  • Aquatic ecosystems and marine life
  • Water quality for human consumption
  • Overall environmental health
  • Community well-being and tourism

Traditional manual cleaning methods are time-consuming and often dangerous for workers.

Technical Design

  • Waterproof Chassis: Custom-built boat hull designed for stability and maneuverability
  • Collection System: Front-mounted collection mechanism to gather floating debris
  • Propulsion System: Reliable motor system for navigation in water
  • Remote Control: Wireless control system for safe operation from shore
  • Storage Container: Built-in compartment to store collected trash

Built With

Waterproof Motors Remote Control Chassis Design Mechanical Engineering Environmental Engineering

How It Works

  • Robot is deployed in polluted water bodies
  • Operator controls the robot remotely from safe distance
  • Collection mechanism gathers floating trash and debris
  • Collected waste is stored in onboard container
  • Robot returns to shore for waste disposal and redeployment
